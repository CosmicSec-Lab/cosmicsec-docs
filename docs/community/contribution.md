# Contribution Guidelines

Thank you for your interest in contributing to CosmicSec! We welcome contributions from the community to help make our security ecosystem even better.

## 🤝 How to Contribute

### 1. Reporting Bugs
If you find a bug, please open an issue in the relevant repository. Include:
*   A clear, descriptive title.
*   Steps to reproduce the bug.
*   Expected vs. actual behavior.
*   Your environment details (OS, versions, etc.).

### 2. Suggesting Features
We love hearing new ideas! Please open an issue and tag it as a `feature request`. Explain why the feature is needed and how it would benefit the ecosystem.

### 3. Submitting Code
1.  **Fork** the repository you want to contribute to.
2.  **Create a branch** for your changes.
3.  **Implement** your changes and **add tests**.
4.  **Verify** that all tests pass and linting is clean.
5.  **Submit a Pull Request** (PR) to the `main` branch.

Please refer to the [Developer Onboarding Guide](../guides/developer-onboarding.md) for detailed technical standards and local setup instructions.

## 📜 Code of Conduct

All contributors are expected to follow our [Code of Conduct](./code-of-conduct.md). We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for everyone.

## ⚖️ License & Intellectual Property

By contributing to CosmicSec, you agree that your contributions will be licensed under the project's [LICENSE](../../LICENSE). You also represent that you have the right to contribute the code and that it does not infringe on any third-party rights.

## 🏆 Contributor Recognition

We appreciate all our contributors! Significant contributions may be recognized on our website and in the project's release notes.
